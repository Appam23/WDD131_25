The three major fastival in North-East India

BIHU

Bihu (Assam): Bihu marks the Assamese agricultural cycle with three celebrations—Rongali/Bohag (April) for sowing, Kati (October) for crop protection, and Magh (January) for harvest—and features lively folk songs, traditional dances and community feasts. It’s a time for family gatherings, cultural performances and rituals that thank nature and renew social bonds.

HORNBILL

Hornbill Festival (Nagaland): Held in early December, the Hornbill Festival is a week‑long showcase of Naga tribal cultures designed to revive traditions and promote tourism, featuring tribal dances, music, craft exhibitions, food stalls and colorful parades. The festival brings different Naga tribes together for competitions, cultural exchanges and performances that highlight their heritage.

WANGALA

Wangala (Garo Hills, Meghalaya): Wangala, the “Hundred Drums” harvest festival of the Garo people (usually Nov–Dec), is a thanksgiving celebration for a good harvest centered on powerful drumming, flute and pipe music and synchronized group dances in traditional attire. The community gathers for altar offerings, rituals led by priests and large feasts that reinforce social ties and ancestral gratitude.
